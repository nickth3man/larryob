Building a comprehensive basketball statistics platform is a massive but incredibly rewarding engineering project. To replicate that level of depth, you need a tightly structured relational database. You cannot simply store pre-calculated averages; you must store the raw, granular game logs so you can calculate any split, trend, or advanced metric on the fly.

Here is the exact data you need to source and how to structure the core schema.

### 1. The Core Entities (Static Data)

You need to establish the foundational tables before you can start logging stats. These tables act as the central reference points.

**Players Table**

* `player_id` (Primary Key - e.g., 'goberru01')
* `first_name`
* `last_name`
* `birth_date`
* `height` & `weight`
* `position` (PG, SG, SF, PF, C)
* `draft_year`

**Teams Table**

* `team_id` (Primary Key - e.g., 'MIN')
* `city`
* `nickname` (e.g., 'Timberwolves')
* `conference` & `division`

---

### 2. The Transactional Data (Dynamic Data)

This is the lifeblood of the platform. You need a definitive record of every game and the performance of every player in that specific game.

**Games Table**

* `game_id` (Primary Key)
* `season_id` (e.g., '2025-26')
* `game_date`
* `home_team_id` & `away_team_id` (Foreign Keys)
* `home_score` & `away_score`
* `season_type` (Regular Season, Playoffs, Play-In)

**Player_Game_Logs (The Box Score)**
This table will be the largest in your database. It holds one row per player, per game.

* `game_id` & `player_id` (Composite Primary Key)
* `team_id` (To track players who are traded mid-season)
* `minutes_played` (MIN)
* `fgm`, `fga` (Field Goals Made/Attempted)
* `fg3m`, `fg3a` (3-Pointers Made/Attempted)
* `ftm`, `fta` (Free Throws Made/Attempted)
* `oreb`, `dreb`, `reb` (Offensive, Defensive, Total Rebounds)
* `ast`, `stl`, `blk`, `tov`, `pf`, `pts` (Assists, Steals, Blocks, Turnovers, Personal Fouls, Points)
* `plus_minus` (+/-)

**Awards Table**
To track historical accolades and career achievements.

* `award_id` (Primary Key)
* `player_id` (Foreign Key)
* `award_name` (e.g., MVP, DPOY, ROY)
* `season_id`

*Structuring your data this way makes fact-checking and aggregations incredibly simple. For instance, verifying how many DPOY awards Rudy Gobert has (he has 4) becomes a straightforward `COUNT` operation filtered by his `player_id`.*

### 4. Implementation Strategy

When extracting this data, writing a custom scraper in Python (or utilizing existing wrappers like `basketball_reference_web_scraper` or the `nba_api` package) and loading the results directly into DuckDB is a highly efficient way to handle the massive volume of analytical queries needed for a stats site. You can easily version control your schema definitions and data ingestion scripts in git as the project evolves.
